Admissions

Admissions to our Computer Science program are designed to select highly motivated and academically proficient individuals who are passionate about pursuing a career in technology. The process involves the following steps:

  1. Application Submission: Prospective students must submit a detailed application form, providing information about their academic background, extracurricular activities, and a personal statement outlining their interest in computer science.

    2. Academic Criteria: Applicants are required to obtain a minimum of B3 in Core Mathematics in the West African Senior School Certificate Examination (WASSCE) and maintain a total aggregate not exceeding grade 12. These criteria ensure a strong foundation in mathematics, a fundamental aspect of computer science.

    Entrance Examination: Shortlisted candidates may be required to take an entrance examination to assess their problem-solving and analytical skills. This examination helps us identify individuals with the potential to excel in the program.

    Interview: Successful candidates from the entrance examination phase will be invited for an interview. The interview provides an opportunity for the admissions committee to better understand the applicant's motivation, goals, and suitability for the program.

    Final Selection: The final selection is based on a holistic evaluation of the application, academic performance, entrance examination results, and interview performance.

We believe that this comprehensive admissions process ensures that our students not only have a strong academic foundation but also possess the qualities needed to thrive in the dynamic field of computer science.
